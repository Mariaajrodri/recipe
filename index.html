How to make Venezuelan Arepas

Ingredients to make the Arepa dough

• 4 Cups of P.A.N. pre-cooked white corn meal
• 4 Cups of water
• 1/4 Cup of Canola Oil (any brand)
• 1 Tablespoon of Salt
• PAM Original Canola Oil Blend

Ingredients you can stuff the Arepa with after cooked


Honey Ham
Salami
Chedder Cheese
Cheese Wiz
Philadelphia Cream Cheese
Avocado
Shredded Meat
Shredded Cheese

  1. Preheat your pan skillet or electric skillet to 400°F.
  2. Have the 4 cups of corn meal, 4 cups of water, 1/4 cup of Canola Oil, and 1 Tablespoon of Salt ready to mix into a big bowl. Add each item by order as said above.
  3. Make sure you wash your hands before touching the dough.
  4. You will start massaging and mixing everything together in the bowl. The goal is to get rid of the little balls that get formed. Similar to mash potatoes and trying to get rid of the concentrated areas.
  5. Keep massaging the dough till you have no residue on your hands from mixing or on the sides of the bowl.
  6. You will go back to your pan or electric skillet and spray PAM Original Canola Oil Blend. Cover all the heating areas.
  7. Have your bowl with the Arepa dough right next to you and your heated skillet.
  8. Grab a handful of dough and create a ball that fits in-between the palm of your hands. Think like you're creating a snowball. Pat the dough ball around fixing all deformity.
  9. Now, you will flatten that dough ball into a flat pancake. Pat the edges of the flattened dough all around circling the pancaked dough against your hands.
  10. After all edges are smooth on the flattened dough, place it on the heated skillet.
  11. Let each side of the Arepa cook till each side is golden with a touch of grill burn.
  12. You will keep grabbing handfulls of dough and repeating steps 7-10.
  13. On a pan skillet you will most likely only place 1 or 2 Arepa doughs to cook. On a electric skillet, you might be able to place about 6 in rows of 3.
  • You can make them FRIED too! Follow the same steps and instead place them on a pan with hot oil, flip each side.
  • If you saved some, to reheat you will reheat them on the skillet again. Spray the PAM oil blend or add butter to the pan and place the arepa till you feel each side warm with just placing your finger to feel the temperature.
  • For a healthier taste and feel, use vegetable oil.
  • You can have them for breakfast, lunch, or dinner depending what you add to the inside. For example. for breakfast you can add a over medium egg with ham and cheese.
